Explore the World's Seven Wonders

Great Wall of China

Built over 2,000 years ago, the Great Wall of China stretches approximately 13,170 miles from east to west of China, protecting the ancient Chinese empire from invasions.

Petra

Known as the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone, Petra was carved into the rose-colored sandstone cliffs by the Nabataeans around 312 BCE and served as their capital city.

Christ the Redeemer

Standing at 98 feet tall on top of Corcovado Mountain in Rio de Janeiro, this Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ was completed in 1931 and has become an emblematic symbol of Brazil.

Taj Mahal

Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, this ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India was completed in 1643 and is considered a masterpiece of Mughal architecture.

Colosseum

Built in 80 AD, this massive amphitheater in Rome could hold up to 80,000 spectators who gathered to watch gladiatorial contests, mock sea battles, animal hunts, and executions.

Machu Picchu

This 15th-century Inca citadel sits on a mountain ridge 7,970 feet above sea level in Peru. It was built around 1450 but abandoned a century later during the Spanish Conquest.

Pyramids of Giza

The oldest of the Seven Wonders, these ancient Egyptian pyramids were built over 4,500 years ago as tombs for pharaohs. The Great Pyramid, built for Pharaoh Khufu, stood as the tallest man-made structure for nearly 4,000 years.
